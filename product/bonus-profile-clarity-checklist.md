# Bonus: 5-Minute Profile Clarity Checklist

Give this to every buyer with their delivery. It makes the $5 offer feel more useful and helps them apply the rewrite.

## 1. The 5-second test

Ask someone to look at your profile for 5 seconds, then answer:

- Who is this for?
- What do they post or sell?
- What should I do next?

If they cannot answer, the profile is still too vague.

## 2. Bio formula

Use one of these:

```text
I help [specific person] get [specific result] with [method].
[Simple proof or personality signal].
[One CTA].
```

```text
[What you make/do] for [specific audience].
[Why it matters].
[One CTA].
```

## 3. Remove vague words

Replace words like:

- helping people
- grow
- level up
- success
- lifestyle
- value

With specific words:

- home workouts for busy beginners
- short videos for local shops
- Notion systems for students
- product photos for handmade sellers

## 4. Use one CTA

Pick one:

- Follow for [topic].
- DM "[word]" for [thing].
- Book a quick call.
- Read the pinned post.
- Shop the link.

Do not ask for three actions at once.

## 5. Pin one starter post

Good starter post formats:

- Start here if you are new.
- I help [audience] do [result].
- 3 mistakes I keep seeing in [niche].
- My simple method for [result].
- What I would do first if I were [audience].

## 6. Match the profile

Your bio, profile photo, banner, and pinned post should all point to the same idea. If each part says something different, visitors hesitate.

## 7. Do not invent proof

Use real proof only:

- Your own experience.
- A real result.
- A real project.
- A real review with permission.
- A clear point of view.

No fake testimonials, fake numbers, or income claims.
